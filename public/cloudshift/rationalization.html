Overview

Most Mergers and Acquisitions create a conglomerate of systems from the original organizations that have overlapping, duplicate and redundant functionality; a situation that drives unnecessary costs and complexity for the organization. In addition to the increased costs, the resulting complexity introduces roadblocks for the implementation of strategic customer initiatives that lean on the accessing key data and functionality embedded in this complexity.
The prospect of rationalizing these IT systems and retiring those no longer needed components, presents formidable challenges:

  • How to convince the business or stakeholders of the need to consolidating a system through
    • Simplifying application footprint
    • Enabling applications to take advantage of Cloud platforms
    • Realizing cost savings
    • Reduced operational risk moving forwards
    • Accelerating the time to market for strategic initiatives
  • How to approach a rationalization and decommissioning project
    • Where do you start?
    • What process will you follow to ensure that a thorough plan is created?
  • How do you control and execute change and contain the risk of disruption to other live services?

Many enterprises take a quick decision to not decommission old systems properly; however, there is a risk that the continued presence of dormant hardware, environments and unused but deployed code are potentially driving hidden costs that could amount to significant annual losses to the organization, not to mention posing an ongoing security risk if not adequately monitored and maintained.

The IBM Rationalization for Cloud method provides a global, standard, repeatable, predictable shared service that delivers the range of services, tools, and skills necessary to consolidate and decommission applications, interfaces and infrastructure. A flexible, scalable rationalization process will be a substantial driver of cost reduction across the organization as well as an enabler for business and process change.

Quickstart

An IBM has considerable experience establishing and running Legacy consolidation and decommissioning factories but believe that the breadth of this vision and capability will be unique to each client. A factory like process can be established in an incremental fashion, complementing client’s capabilities, while aiding where a formalization and acceleration of processes can bring the most benefits to the client.

It is expected that the Factory will initially provide a limited range of services to support a limited range of applications. So, for example this could be Data Consolidation, Interface Decommissioning or Application Decommissioning processes for a sub-set of client applications.

The Factory must however have the governance, processes and structures to quickly and reliably respond to demand. This demand could include quickly increasing capacity for existing services or support for new services in new geographies.

The following is an example of a Decommissioning Factory model that brought immediate value to the financial services client, value realized in less than six months.

A large commercial bank faced a difficult situation in their 3-year application decommissioning program

In Q3 2015 IBM was engaged to set up a scalable factory service to manage the decommission process, with the following results. The IBM Decommissioning Factory delivered a 75% increase in throughput, enabling the bank to exceed its stretch target for 2015.

FAQ's

How is CloudShift Migrate to Cloud different than server replication, scripting, templates and manual migration?

CloudShift -> Migrate to cloud is a predictable migration solution that automates the application information collection, identifies the application dependency and migrate of application workload and their associated metadata/configuration into cloud. Step-by-step migration methods offers automated migration of workloads without any pre-processing on the source environment or post-processing on the target cloud to minimize business disruption.

What is the basic difference between migrating application workloads and servers? Aren't they the same?

Application installed in servers containing the OS, the applications and platforms (e.g. application servers, databases) associated with the workload. Application Workloads typically include the entire suite (servers), data storage (data may be stored on attached and/or networked storage) and the network and system configurations associated with the workload. Our migrate to cloud method ensures application entire workload can be migrated seamlessly with minimum business intervention.

Does CloudShift Migrate to cloud supports migrate to SaaS?

Yes – Our analytical tools supports to identify the application source pattern to create the right target cloud pattern and execute the migrate process according to the best suitable pattern to avoid business disruption while taking vantage of cloud.

Does CloudShift Migrate to cloud require a proprietary environment to import application information into prior to migration to cloud platform ?

It does need a transient environment to support the conversion before application workloads are deployed into the target cloud platform. This is only required if application workload needs conversion e.g. platform upgrade or remediation etc. Our migrate toolset is capable of hosted either in client environment or target cloud platform to pull the application binaries, data accordingly to network connection used between source and target cloud connection.

What does it mean to migrate a server live?

It means that production servers can be migrated without having to take them down out of production during the migration. Once the migration is complete and the workload is configured and tested on the target cloud environment, the client can set up a change window to cut-over to the new VM deployed in the target data cloud.

What Platforms Does Migrate to cloud support?

It supports migration from physical, virtual and cloud platforms to cloud platforms. Supported OS, hypervisors and Clouds are:

  • Source OS – Windows 2003/2008/2012, Linux, AIX
  • Target Clouds – IBM Bluemix, AWS VPC, VMWare vCloud, Azure or private cloud platform.

Do I need to install anything discover application information ? What about agents?

Our discovery toolset is an agentless technology, so you will not need to install agents. We can also leverage existing client discovery tool and repository to pull the data out and load into our SCOPE analytics to understand application source environment and it's common pattern to derive the appropriate migration path.

How it's different than other Cloud migration service ?

Yes, our migration capabilities are more geared with enterprise application assessment, creating the right migration roadmap to minimise client risk. Our large toolset supporting migration assessment, planning and execution is end-2-end automated to get a most predictable outcome in a short period of time. Migrating the workload is no-brainier today, we differentiate ourself in migrating the right workload at the right cloud platform to take the vantage multi-cloud environment of a hybrid cloud solution for our client. We make sure our client leverages our migration roadmap to plan their application portfolio appropriately to spend their budget most wisely.

Solution and Delivery Guidance

To get the best ROI from the decommissioning of a suite of highly integrated applications it may be required to identify duplicate and overlapping applications supporting specific business processes, consolidating data for a targeted application to be the primary supporting source of the process, remove or re-architect existing interfaces, archive data for targeted for retiring applications taking into account retention policies, decommission the application and then decommission the underlying server and network infrastructure.

Critical Success Factors for industrializing the Factory Model

  • Profiling and estimating the work required for each application
  • Integrate and perform detailed decommission planning as early as possible
  • Engage appropriate stakeholders at each step and escalate through governance
  • Capture and validate key data points, including business rules and policies for the retention of data
  • Minimize impacts on bank resources and SMEs wherever possible
  • Streamline inputs and dependencies (templates, processes, teams)
  • The factory has clear entry and exit criteria defined
